Multivariate Analysis of York Region’s Broader Public Sector Energy Consumption Efficiency across Annual Time Series

Alex Tan

Introduction

Increasingly, cities utilize about 70% of comprehensive energy supply and planned proposals will house nearly 60% of world’s population by 2030 (Seto and Dhakal 2014). Local government buildings provide initial testbeds for any policy initiatives of public sector and private sector before city-wide scale implementation.

Spatially, the York Region of Toronto and Buffalo-Niagara Falls Metropolitan Area have approximately the same size that qualifies as a large settlement area with a humid continental climate. Several policy initiatives on public buildings has been implemented on an ongoing basis or planned and a broad spectrum mapping are visualized.

Temporally, the data sets are provided in an open data license by the York Region municipal with an annual time series. The project will intend to visualize changes across time and conjecture predictive analytics.

As public sites provide supporting activities to sustain the function of a city, the project will query for efficiency in energy consumption through site clustering in broader public sector of York Region. The project will be objective on clustering effect and its’ efficiency on energy consumption and benchmarked compared to municipal government guideline.
